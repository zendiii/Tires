The goal is to create a premium online tire shopping experience that’s simple, modern, and visually appealing. I don’t want it to feel like Tire Rack or Discount Tire where you’re immediately overwhelmed with filters and information. I want it to have a clean, luxury automotive feel with smooth animations and lots of white space, almost like configuring a car on Porsche or Tesla’s website.

The website needs to support two main ways of shopping:

1. Search by Vehicle

The customer enters:

Year
Make
Model
Trim

The website should automatically know the OEM tire size and always recommend the factory size first. After that, it can show popular upgrade sizes if they exist.

Instead of immediately throwing a huge product list at the customer, I’d like it to feel more interactive.

One idea I really like is displaying a close-up of the vehicle’s wheel and tire instead of the whole car. As different tires or wheel sizes are selected, the wheel/tire visualization updates. I think this keeps the focus on what we’re actually selling.

If the vehicle has a staggered setup (Corvette, Porsche, BMW M cars, etc.), the interface should automatically adapt and show both the front and rear wheels with their respective tire sizes.

2. Search by Tire Size

For customers who already know their tire size, there should be a separate tab where they can search directly by size (ex: 275/55R20) without needing to enter their vehicle.

This page should be optimized for speed with:

Tire size search
Brand filters
Tire type
Load range
Price filters
Product list

I don’t think this page needs a vehicle visualizer since these customers already know what they’re looking for.

Tire Results

I’d like the results to be clean and easy to compare.

Each tire card should show:

Brand
Model
Tire image
Price
Mileage warranty
Rating
Availability
“View Details”
“Add to Cart”

I also want the OEM recommendation clearly labeled so customers immediately know which option is the factory replacement.

Mobile Installation

Since I own the tire department and also offer mobile installation, I’d like that integrated throughout the experience.

Instead of just adding tires to a cart, customers should be able to choose:

Ship to home
Mobile installation
In-shop installation

Eventually I’d also like appointment scheduling built directly into checkout.

Future Features

Long term I’d like to add:

Customer accounts
Saved vehicles (“My Garage”)
Tire rotation reminders
Fleet accounts
VIN lookup
TPMS information
Fitment guides
Wheel visualizer
AI-powered photo upload where customers can preview wheels/tires on their own vehicle (this is definitely a future phase).

Design

I want to keep the design clean and premium.

Black or light gray backgrounds with orange accents (matching Dino’s branding), large automotive photography/renders, smooth transitions, and minimal clutter.

One thing we’ve discussed that I really like is using progressive disclosure. Instead of showing everything at once, the site guides the customer through the buying process step by step so it never feels overwhelming.

Long-Term Goal

This isn’t just a website—I want it to become a complete online tire platform that can eventually sell tires nationwide while also supporting my local mobile installation business.

I know this is a big project and I don’t expect all of it to be built at once. I’d rather build it in phases with a solid foundation than rush everything into version one.
